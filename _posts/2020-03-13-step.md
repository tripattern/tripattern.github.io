### Introduction
One of the challenges many people face is being able to focus.
Our days are often filled with distractions of our own doing (Social Media...)
and distractions that come from others (meetings upon meetings upon meetings...).
How do we find ways to focus on specific tasks and ensure we give that task the undividided attentio it requires?

Many people have offered solutions to this problem. Some solutions include:
* [Pomodoro Technique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pomodoro_Technique)
* [Deep Work](https://www.calnewport.com/books/deep-work/)
* [Getting Things Done](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Getting_Things_Done)
* [The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_7_Habits_of_Highly_Effective_People)

I wanted to find a solution that learns from the patterns of others, but also takes into account the challenegs of
working within or on complex systems. I came to realize that what I needed was actually an agile process reduced to
a time box of one hour.
